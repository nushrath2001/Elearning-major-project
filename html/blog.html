The Rise Of E-learning During Covid-19 pandemic

Due to the COVID-19 pandemic, education has significantly changed.
The global pandemic has drastically changed the way students are learning worldwide and thus distinctive online learning has taken place. Students from all around the world have suddenly shifted from classroom learning to online learning and some are even wondering whether such change will continue even after the global pandemic is over.
Investing in education technology is imperative.
It is visible more than ever that investment in education technology as well as its integration in schools is highly important in order to provide a seamless transition to online learning to students. Therefore, in response to the COVID-19 pandemic it is essential for educational institutions to provide their students with a user-friendly online learning platform in order to sustain quality learning.
Increase in the demand for blended learning.
There is a high chance that after the pandemic there will be an increase in the demand for a hybrid learning model and thus a combination of online and campus-based learning will emerge, also known as blended learning. Numerous research has also identified online learning to be even more efficient in some cases than classroom learning. For example, online learning allows students to learn at a quicker speed by learning at their own pace, re-reading and re-taking the pre-recorded lectures as well as they have the opportunity to go through the topics as they choose in some cases.

Eat Sleep Code And Repeat

The “Eat, sleep, code, repeat” phrase is intended to be a way of saying “coding is awesome and we want to do it all the time!”. I get the point, I appreciate the enthusiasm, I do.
Have you heard of the statement “programmers have no life?” that is absolutely wrong! Programmers do have a life.
Programmers have numerous interests and talents far outside their ability code. It could be reading, art, hiking, running, writing, spending time in nature, or just hanging out with family and friends.
A programmer needs to have a truly balanced lifestyle — one that gives the brain some space to breath non-programming air. Life outside of code helps nurture important qualities and skills: inspiration, creativity, patience, flexibility and many more. All of these skills make you a better programmer and you can not acquired them by just coding.
In summary Don’t believe the hype that if you don’t spend all your night awake or non-eating hours programming, you’ll not be a good programmer.
If you want to become the very best programmer you can be, make out time for some non-coding activities. Let your brain stretch it’s legs and breath some non-programming air.
“Eat, sleep, code, repeat”.
In essence, the main takeaways are as following: 1. Don’t get caught up in your own thoughts, collaborate with your team, share your thoughts.
2. Understanding that people come from different backgrounds and that communication is important.
3. Running your thoughts by people outside of your team can introduce a fresh approach to your problems.
