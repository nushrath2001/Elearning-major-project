Course Overview

In general terms, the word “graphic” refers to any visual representation of data and includes a variety of forms including drawings, photographs, line art, graphs, diagrams, numbers, symbols, geometric designs, maps, and engineering drawings. Graphics may be images only or may also include text and they can be used for decorative or functional purposes, including educating, informing, illustrating, or entertaining.

What you'll learn

graphic design from scratch - beginner to advanced.

Web Publishing: Students develop their skills in website design, implementation and maintenance as well as honing their skills in using HTML and website publishing programs, such as DreamWeaver.

Computer Graphic Design: This course improves students' skills using photo editing and paint programs as well as their basic computer and technical skills.

Visual Design: Visual design allows students to exercise their creative thought, color theory and visual presentation skills.
